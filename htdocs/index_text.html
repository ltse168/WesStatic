Introduction

The Final Register of Electors for Rural Representative Election is published in October every year. If you are eligible for registration as an elector and wish to have your registration particulars included in this year’s Register, you should submit your application for registration on or before 16 July of this year. If the application form reaches the Home Affairs Department (HAD)/Electoral Registration Officer (ERO) after 16 July of this year, information provided therein will be processed when the provisional registers of the following year are compiled.

You may perform a search on this “Rural Representative Election Voter Registration Information Enquiry System” (the System) to see if you have been registered as an elector.

If you are a registered elector, you may perform a search on the System to enquire about the Rural Committee and Village/Market Town for which you have been registered as an elector in the 2020 Final Register of Electors.

If you have submitted the Application for New Voter Registration, you may also perform a search on the System to enquire about the status of your application.

Please be reminded that the System is provided for electors to check their own registered particulars only. Anyone who checks another elector’s registered particulars through the System without the latter’s prior written consent may contravene the relevant provisions of the Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance (Cap. 486).

A registered elector of Indigenous Inhabitant Representative Election who wishes to report on change of his/her principal particulars (i.e. name) contained in the Final Register of Electors should submit his/her application on or before 16 June of this year. As for the request for change of other particulars (including principal residential address, correspondence address and the Indigenous Village/Composite Indigenous Village as registered), the application should be submitted on or before 16 July of this year.

A registered elector of Resident Representative Election/Kaifong Representative Election who wishes to report on change of his/her principal particulars (i.e. name or principal residential address) contained in the Final Register of Electors should submit his/her application on or before 16 June of this year. Please note that a registered elector of Resident Representative Election/Kaifong Representative Election applying for change of principal residential address (regardless of whether the principal residential address is still within the boundary of the Existing Village/Market Town as registered) must submit together with the application form the required proof of principal residential address. For details, please refer to the Guidance Notes attached to the application form.

An elector who wishes to report on change of his or her registered particulars should complete and sign the Application for Change of Registered Particulars and submit the form to the HAD/ERO by the following means:

  1. by post (Address: Home Affairs Department, 30/F, Southorn Centre, 130 Hennessy Road, Wan Chai, Hong Kong);
  2. by fax (Fax no.: 2591 6392);
  3. in person or by a representative to the HAD Headquarters or Home Affairs Enquiry Centres of District Offices;
  4. by email (Email address: rre@had.gov.hk), with the signed application form scanned and attached as an email attachment; or
  5. by using the electronic form and uploading the completed and signed form to the dedicated website of HAD.

Applications received by the HAD/ERO after the above deadlines will be processed when the provisional registers of the following year are compiled.

Regulations to be observed in using the system:

Under section 32(3) of the Electoral Affairs Commission (Registration of Electors) Regulation (Cap. 541K), a person who -

  1. reproduces or permits another person to reproduce in any form any particular contained in an entry in a register or in an extract from a register;
  2. uses or permits another person to use any information relating to a person contained in an entry in a register or in an extract from a register; or
  3. imparts to any other person any information referred to in paragraph (a) and (b) above,
for a purpose other than a purpose related to an election, commits an offence and is liable to a fine at level 2 and to imprisonment for 6 months.

“Voter Registration Information Enquiry System” (System) only allows an elector to check his or her own registered particulars. Anyone who checks another elector’s registered particulars through the System without the latter’s prior written consent may contravene the relevant provisions of the Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance (Cap. 486).

Privacy Policy

Privacy Policy

Home Affairs Department (HAD) is concerned to ensure that all personal data submitted through HAD website are handled in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance.

HAD will record visits to HAD website without collecting any personal identifiable information of users. Such general statistics are collected for the compilation of statistical reports and the diagnosis of problems with or concerning computer systems to help HAD improve HAD website.

For searching Village Representative Election voter registration information through the HAD "Village Representative Election (VRE)" website, users are requested to input personal data. Personal information includes name and identity document number. HAD will specify the collection purpose and intended usage of your data provided and advise you how you may request access to or correction of the personal data provided for the purpose of voter registration.

"Search for VRE Voter Registration Information" website uses SSL protocol to encrypt data during network transmission to protect your personal data. All personal data you provide to HAD are secured, and access to them is restricted to authorized personnel only.

Search service on HAD website is provided by Yahoo! Hong Kong Limited ("Yahoo!"). We are advised by Yahoo! that it does not collect personal identifiable information while serving search results through HAD website. Yahoo! will share the anonymous data it collects through the search service with the Government for compiling traffic analysis on government websites. The Government will not match the data obtained from any such search activity with any personal data possibly held by the Government.

This Privacy Policy has been translated into Chinese. If there is any inconsistency or ambiguity between the English version and the Chinese version, the English version shall prevail.

Technical Configuration

This website is best viewed in 800x600 resolution with Microsoft Internet Explorer 9 (or above).

All pages of this website are in Unicode (UTF-8) encoding. In case the browser cannot recognise the encoding automatically (thus making the text illegible), appropriate encoding should be selected to display the text : point the mouse to the window/frame in question and select Unicode (UTF-8) from the browser menu (usually in the View-Encoding sub-menu).

To view the most updated version of a web page, it is sometimes necessary to refresh/reload the page in question (e.g. by selecting View-Refresh from the Internet Explorer menu or pressing<Ctrl><F5>). If the above doesn't work, try clearing the cache (e.g. by selecting Tools-Internet Options from the Internet Explorer menu, click the Temporary Internet files-Delete files button on the General tab in the pop-up dialogue box, and restart Internet Explorer).

"Special Chinese Characters" and "Hong Kong Supplementary Character Set" are required for viewing some of the Chinese characters.

Personal information Collection Statement

Purpose of Collection

Personal data provided by you will be used by HAD only for the purpose of handling your enquiry.

Your enquiry may also be used for the compilation of statistics on the number and nature of enquiries received by HAD. The outcome of the resulting statistics will not be in a form that will identify your enquiry and any of your personal data.

The provision of your personal data is voluntary. If you do not provide sufficient information, HAD may not be able to process your enquiries.

 

Classes of Transferees

Where necessary, the particulars submitted by you may be provided to other departments/organisations/persons for the purposes of voter registration and election under relevant Ordinances and /or subsidiary legislation and all purposes related thereto.

 

Access to Personal Data

You have a right to request access to, and to request the correction of, your personal data and other related information you provided for the purpose of voter registration in accordance with the provisions of the Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance.  Such requests should be made in writing to the Electoral Registration Officer at 30/F, Southorn Centre, 130 Hennessy Road, Wanchai, Hong Kong.
